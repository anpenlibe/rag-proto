# Legal requirements

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/ai-in-your-studies-what-is-permitted/legal-requirements-data-protection-and-copyright/*

In addition to the requirements arising from the learning outcomes [Lernzielen](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/ai-in-your-studies-what-is-permitted/basic-framework/#c1268367), the basic [rules of good scientific practice](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/good-scientific-practice/#c1269035) and the requirements of the respective teacher, regulations on data protection and copyright are also relevant when dealing with AI.

## Copyright: Protection of intellectual property and AI-generated content

Copyright is vital for protecting the intellectual creations of authors and ensuring that they have the autonomy to determine how their works are used.

- In accordance with prevailing legal principles, the authorship of a work is recognised as belonging exclusively to human beings and not to AI systems.
- Although AI is not granted its own copyright to a text, AI-generated content may not be presented as one's own academic work (this is also prohibited by the terms of use of AI tools).

Please be aware that uploading your own or third-party texts to AI tools can also present challenges. This is due to the fact that it may involve disclosing copyright-protected content without having control over how this material will be further processed by the tools.

- If the AI tool does not use the uploaded texts for training purposes, uploading them should fall under the right to private copying ([Section 42 of the German Copyright Act (UrhG)](https://www.ris.bka.gv.at/GeltendeFassung.wxe?Abfrage=Bundesnormen&Gesetzesnummer=10001848)).
- However, it is not permitted to upload entire books or magazines to AI tools.
- Material that has obviously been produced in an unlawful manner may also not be uploaded to AI tools.
- You must not circumvent any technical copy protection measures.

Therefore, please note that you may only upload copyright-protected material to an AI tool for private copying purposes if the data is not used to train new AI models. [U:ai](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/uai-the-university-of-viennas-ai-tool/) is a tool that does not use your data for training purposes.

## Data protection: Processing personal and confidential data in AI tools

During their studies, students often work with sensitive or confidential information, such as interview data, survey results, personal details or internal documents. It is imperative that this data is handled in accordance with the relevant regulations, particularly the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Particular caution is required when using AI tools, as it is often impossible to trace where entered content is stored or further processed. In particular, the following data should not be entered into AI tools without protection:

- personal data of third parties, obtained for example through interviews, surveys or courses;
- confidential research data or internal documents;
- text drafts or scientific work, if their intended use is unclear;
- sensitive information that allows conclusions to be drawn about individual persons.

If there is any uncertainty regarding the manner in which an AI tool processes data, it is advisable to refrain from uploading the data. Please refer to the information provided on [u:ai](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/uai-the-university-of-viennas-ai-tool/).

Students must ensure that they comply with copyright law and that third-party content is correctly labelled. They must also ensure that AI-supported contributions are not presented as their own work.
