# Making up for supplementary examinations from the bachelor programme for admission to a master programme

*Section: Admission · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/admission/non-degree-programme/making-up-for-subject-specific-supplementary-examinations-from-the-bachelor-programme/*

The admission to master programmes is possible with supplementary examinations to an extent of up to 30 ECTS to compensate for fundamental subject-specific differences. If this extent is exceeded, your application for admission will be rejected.

Your application for admission was rejected for this reason? You can make up for lacking supplementary examinations in a non-degree programme, until 30 ECTS or less remain. For this purpose, you will be admitted to "attending individual courses", not to a specific degree programme.

For example: You lack 39 ECTS to compensate for fundamental subject-specific differences. Therefore, your application is rejected. Subsequently, you complete supplementary examinations to an extent of 9 ECTS or more as a non-degree student.  Afterwards, you can apply for admission to the degree programme again within the [application period](/en/admission/application-and-admission-periods/).

If the supplementary examination in German was prescribed in your admission letter, please read the information regarding the [University Preparation Programme of the Vienna Universities (VWU)](/en/admission/non-degree-programme/university-preparation-programme-of-the-vienna-universities-vwu/) carefully.

## Consequences of a non-degree programme

- Examination attempts in the non-degree programme also count for the degree programme. If all examination attempts are graded negatively in the non-degree programme, you are permanently excluded from the degree programme and cannot be admitted to this programme at the University of Vienna.
- You have to pay the [tuition fee as well as the Students' Union fee](/en/tuition-fee/), irrespective of your citizenship.
- You are not admitted to a specific degree programme, but to attending individual courses ("Besuch einzelner Lehrveranstaltungen").
- The admission to a non-degree programme is possibile until the end of the admission period. However, the registration deadlines for courses end before the beginning of the semester in some cases. Therefore, it is possible that you miss these deadlines.
- Students admitted to a degree programme are favoured in the allocation of course places. If you have any questions regarding the registration for courses and examinations, please contact the [StudiesServiceCenter or StudiesServiceUnit](https://www.univie.ac.at/en/about-us/governance-structure/organisation-of-studies-courses/) of the degree programme in which the courses are offered.
- You are only allowed to take individual examinations/courses and must comply with the curriuclum's registration requirements. Furthermore, you are not allowed to take any public defenses. Graduating is not possible.
- There might be restrictions/limitations when it comes to health insurance, residence permit and financial assistance. Please make an inquiry at the responsible authority about the consequences of your non-degree programme up front.

## Admission to a non-degree programme

1. Register in u:space
   If this is your first time studying at the University of Vienna: Activate your u:account in [u:space](https://uspace.univie.ac.at/en/web/gast/) within the application period. Make sure you remember the login data, because you will further need the u:account during your studies. Log in to u:space, complete the registration.
2. Apply for admission to a non-degree programme
   Log into the [Servicedesk](https://servicedesk.univie.ac.at/plugins/servlet/desk/portal/61/create/1644) with your u:account and let us know that you want to be admitted to a non-degree programme.
3. Wait for an answer
   Once you have been (preliminarily) admitted, you will receive an e-mail containing important information. Please read this e-mail carefully. Please note: The University of Vienna reserves the right to invite you to an [online appointment](/en/admission/online-first-time-admission/).
4. Pay the tuition fee/Students' Union fee.
   The admission is completed as soon as the [prescribed fee](/en/tuition-fee/) is received in u:space > Financial Matters > Tuition fee/Students' Union fee. Afterwards, you can download your study documents (student record sheet and confirmation of enrolment) in u:space > Personal matters > My documents.

### Creating a semester schedule and ordering the u:card

As soon as your tuition fee has been received by the University of Vienna, you can validate your student ID card, the [u:card](http://ucard.univie.ac.at/en) or order it if you do not yet have one.

Register for the required courses. The responsible [StudiesServiceCenter (SSC)/ StudiesServiceUnit (SSS)](https://www.univie.ac.at/ueber-uns/leitung-organisation/studien-lehrorganisation/) will gladly help you with any questions regarding registration for courses and examination.

## What happens next?

Complete the required supplementary examinations in the non-degree programme. Only supplementary examinations to the extent of 30 ECTS or less remain? Apply for admission to the desired [master programme](/en/admission-procedure/master-programmes/) in u:space within the [application period](/en/admission/application-and-admission-periods/). Your application will be processed according to the prevailing legal status at that time.

## FAQ

/

- How can I switch to the degree programme?

  You have already completed examinations in the non-degree programme and supplementary examinations to the extent of 30 ECTS or less remain? Apply for admission to the desired [master programme](/en/admission-procedure/master-programmes/) in u:space within the [application period](/en/admission/application-and-admission-periods/). Your application will be processed according to the prevailing legal status at that time.
- I am not able to register for a course. How shall I proceed?

  Please contact the [StudiesServiceCenter or StudiesServiceUnit](https://www.univie.ac.at/en/about-us/governance-structure/organisation-of-studies-courses/) of the degree programme in which the course is offered.
- Which supplementary examinations do I have to make up for?

  You can find the required supplementary examinations in your rejection letter. If you have any questions, please contact the [StudiesServiceCenter (SSC) or StudiesServiceUnit (SSS)](https://www.univie.ac.at/ueber-uns/leitung-organisation/studien-lehrorganisation/) in which the course if offered.
