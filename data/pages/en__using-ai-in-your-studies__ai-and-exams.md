# AI and Exams

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/ai-and-exams/*

AI can provide useful support during studies and exam preparation, for example by structuring the learning process, revision, feedback or self-assessment.

However, it is crucial to note that whether and how AI is permitted in examinations is determined by the respective lecturers. Students bear full responsibility for the independence, accuracy and transparency of their work. AI does not replace independent learning or engagement with the subject matter.

#

## Examinations with AI: What does apply here?

At the University of Vienna, an examination is a formal opportunity for students to demonstrate the level of knowledge and skills they have acquired and the achievement of the learning outcomes defined in the curriculum. It is not merely a matter of assessing performance for the award of a grade or academic degree, but also of receiving valuable feedback on learning progress and ensuring fairness and academic integrity. In addition to oral and written examinations, written assignments and presentations also count as examinations.

In the current age of AI, the nature of examinations is evolving. The focus is shifting from mere knowledge reproduction to assessing higher levels of competence, such as reflection, transfer and design. Consequently, emphasis is now on both the final outcome and the individual learner's progress and development.

Whether and how you may use AI in examinations is determined by the respective examiners.

- Where can you find the rules and regulations? The binding provisions specifying which AI tools are permitted for a particular course or examination can be found directly in the course description in u:find. As a general rule, anything not explicitly listed there as permitted is prohibited. If you are unsure, you should always consult your lecturers in order to clarify expectations and framework conditions at an early stage.
- Transparency and documentation: If you are permitted to use AI tools for written assignments, you must [fully disclose](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/citing-and-labelling-ai/#c1269189) such use in accordance with the principles of good academic practice.
- Examination formats are changing: You should be prepared for greater emphasis on competences such as reflection and transfer. To ensure the independence of your performance, written assignments may be supplemented by oral components, plausibility checks or grade-relevant discussions.
- Personal responsibility: It is imperative that you, the author, accept full responsibility for the accuracy, academic validity and integrity of the results submitted. As language models are not "knowledge machines" and may produce [hallucinations](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/what-is-artificial-intelligence/#c1268979), it is essential that all AI-generated content is critically reviewed.
- Consequences of misconduct: The use of unauthorised AI tools constitutes academic misconduct. This results in a non-assessment (entry of an “X”) or the annulment of a grade that has already been awarded. In serious cases, the Rectorate may impose exclusion from studies for up to two semesters.

## AI in exam preparation

AI tools can support exam preparation when used in a targeted and reflective manner. They are not a substitute for independent learning, but they can help to structure, deepen and reflect on the learning process.

### What can AI be used for?

AI tools can be particularly helpful in organising and supporting the learning process. This includes, among other things:

- Reflection on your own time management
- Support in organising learning activities and setting priorities
- Creating and adapting study and revision plans
- Motivation through structured learning steps or gamification elements
- Revising content, for example through questions or study plans on specific topics
- Gaining an overview of key terms, concepts or subject areas

This means that AI can help to structure the learning process, but it does not replace the need to engage with the subject matter.

### How can I use AI tools for learning in practice?

AI tools can support learning in various ways, especially when used specifically for organisation, repetition, feedback and self-assessment. The following examples show typical usage scenarios that students can usefully integrate into their exam preparation.

### Personalised learning platforms

Some learning platforms use AI to provide personalised support for learning processes, adapting them to each user's individual progress.

- Duolingo, for instance, assists language learners in practising regularly, identifying weaknesses, and automatically adapting tasks to their current level.
- [Khan Academy](https://de.khanacademy.org/) offers adaptive exercise formats, particularly in mathematics and science, that respond to tasks that have already been solved.
- Flashcard apps such as [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) can also support learning. They facilitate the creation of flashcards and present them using repetition algorithms at precisely the right time, based on experience, when content should be repeated. This can be particularly beneficial for long-term exam preparation.
- [Notebook LM](https://notebooklm.google.com/) is a highly versatile tool. Here, you can create audio summaries in the form of podcasts, quizzes, flashcards, videos and infographics from documents you have uploaded yourself.

AI supports organisation and repetition here, not actual understanding.

### Active feedback

AI tools can be used as active feedback partners in learning. The AI tool can

- Suggest learning strategies
- Assess learning objectives
- Provide feedback on self-formulated answers
- Ask specific comprehension questions
- Offer suggestions for improvement
- Point out ambiguities or gaps in an explanation
- Present alternative perspectives

Example:

You answer an exam question in writing and then let the AI check whether the answer is coherent, which points are still missing or where terms have been used imprecisely. You remain responsible for the content.

### Intelligent assistance with comprehension questions

Some AI-supported applications assist with specific tasks or with understanding complex content.

- Apps such as [Socrat.ai](https://socrat.ai/) can help with solving various tasks by explaining steps and providing additional resources.
- Tools like Perplexity can be used for initial thematic orientation or rough research.
- Generative AI tools such as ChatGPT or u:ai can be used to explain complex content in simple language, compile key terms in a subject area, or illustrate connections using examples.

## Important information for learning with AI

The use of AI tools can support the learning process, but it also presents challenges. In order to use AI sensibly and responsibly, it is important to consider a few fundamental aspects.

- Due to its [functionality](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/what-is-artificial-intelligence/#c1268970), AI may produce incorrect, incomplete or imprecise information, even if it sounds plausible.
  Therefore, it is advisable to always compare responses with your own materials.
- Subject-specific terminology may be used differently depending on the discipline. When asked to define technical terms, AI generally draws on the meaning most frequently represented in its training data. This may result in definitions that do not correspond to the specific discipline or that lack subject-specific nuances.
  Only adopt definitions or explanations if they align with the academic requirements of your course.
- AI-supported learning tools can heavily structure the learning process, for example through predefined learning paths, automatic revision or recommended content. While this can be helpful, it also entails the risk that your learning process becomes overly externally directed: learning is individual and not every recommendation generated by AI suits your personal learning style.
  Therefore, consciously set your own priorities.
- A common risk factor is increasing dependence on AI tools. If explanations, solution strategies or summaries are regularly delegated to AI, this may impair your ability to think through content independently and to solve problems on your own.
  Especially at the beginning of your studies, it is therefore important to use AI with restraint and to first develop your own learning strategies.

### Tip: Combine digital and analogue learning methods

AI-supported learning is most effective when combined with other forms of learning. Textbooks, lecture notes, lecture materials, videos and exercise sheets remain key learning resources. In addition, taking handwritten notes also promotes the processing of contexts.

The reflective use of AI is a skill that must be learned. This includes understanding the areas in which AI is and isn't suitable, formulating precise and targeted [prompts](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/controlling-ai-in-a-targeted-manner-the-importance-of-prompting-correctly/#c1269203), critically evaluating answers, and taking responsibility for one's own learning process. This approach develops with experience and requires continuous reflection.

Regardless of the tools used, the following applies:

- Responsibility for the learning process, for understanding the content and for the results always lies with the individual.
- AI can support, structure and provide inspiration. However, learning, understanding and assessment remain independent achievements.
