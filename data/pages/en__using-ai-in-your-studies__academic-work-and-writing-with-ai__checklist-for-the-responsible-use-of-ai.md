# Checklist for the responsible use of AI

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/checklist-for-the-responsible-use-of-ai/*

The use of AI in scientific work offers numerous possibilities, but requires conscious and reflective use based on the [principles of good scientific practice](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/good-scientific-practice/#c1269035). Honesty, transparency, independence and traceability are key principles that must also be upheld when working with AI.

### Checklist for the responsible use of AI

General information

- Do not use generative AI for research
- Do not trust AI-generated content; check everything
- Write specific prompts with a clear task, context, and details on format and style
- Do not rely on AI-generated summaries
- Process AI output further
- For each course, check the information in u:find, on Moodle, and information on specific assignments and examinations, and clarify any open questions about the use of AI with the respective lecturer

Privacy Policy & Terms of Use

- Do not enter sensitive or confidential data into AI tools
- Anonymise or avoid entering personal data.
- Obtain the express consent of third parties before processing their data in an AI.
- Ensure that the data you enter is not used for AI training
- Only use GDPR-compliant AI tools that do not store data on servers outside the EU.

Copyright

- Check whether AI-generated texts are based on copyrighted content and cite the authors appropriately
- Ensure that copyrighted texts are reproduced correctly.
- AI does not have copyright to its own output. Nevertheless, you may not pass off the content as your own.
- Bear in mind that texts and documents you enter into AI may themselves be copyrighted: therefore, ensure that your input is not used for AI training.

Honesty & Independence

- Do not pass off AI-generated content as your own work
- Differentiate between Higher Order Concerns (HOCs) and Later Order Concerns (LOCs) – depending on the learning outcomes.

Transparency & traceability

- Document which AI tools you have used, including the version.
- Disclose the purpose and scope of the AI's use.
- If AI-generated content has been copied verbatim, specify the prompt used.
- Check whether detailed documentation of all prompts is necessary or practical.
- If you used AI in a collaborative process, document how it contributed to idea development.
- Only include AI tools in the bibliography if there is a publicly accessible link to the chat.

Using AI as a reflection aid

- Use AI as a Socratic dialogue partner that asks questions and provides food for thought.
- Allow AI to assist you in developing research questions or argumentation structures, without outsourcing your intellectual performance.

Clear distinction between AI support and your own contribution

- Edit and review AI-generated content so that your own scientific voice remains recognisable.
- Ensure that AI only supports the scientific writing process, but does not replace it.
