# Things AI is not recommended for

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/ai-in-your-studies-what-is-permitted/things-ai-is-not-recommended-for/*

While AI has demonstrated remarkable capabilities, there are areas where its use is not recommended, as it could potentially compromise the autonomy of your scientific work or the integrity of your learning processes.

### Acquiring knowledge through AI summaries

AI-generated summaries can provide an overview, but it is important to remember that they do not replace your own engagement with the material. Please note that AI-generated summaries are often also inadequate in terms of content, as they may omit essential aspects or distort statements from the original.

Recommendation: Engage with the topic yourself first or read the texts and then use AI for targeted follow-up questions, for example to clarify aspects you have not understood.

Tip: Do not use AI as a substitute for reading, but as a reading coach.

AI can support you in reading academic texts actively, critically and in a structured manner. For instance, it can provide guiding questions, suggestions for highlighting central arguments, or explanations of individual terms. It is recommended that you read the text yourself first and then use AI for targeted follow-up questions. This will help you to close gaps in understanding or to better contextualise argumentative structures (see tips on [prompting](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/controlling-ai-in-a-targeted-manner-the-importance-of-prompting-correctly/)).

Further information: [AI and reading academic texts](https://ctl.univie.ac.at/fileadmin/user_upload/z_ctl_neu/Angebote_fuer_Studierende/Handouts__Leitfaeden_und_Ressourcen/Handouts_und_Leitfaeden/2024_09_16_AI_and_reading_academic_texts_EN.pdf)

### Tasks that require precision

In tasks where precision is paramount, it is recommended to employ software that operates in a deterministic manner, as opposed to AI models that are probabilistic in nature. Traditional software often proves to be the better choice in certain domains, for example for precise data calculations. For this purpose, you may want to use software such as Excel or SPSS, in addition to other data analysis tools available to you under a [campus licence](http://For).

### Tasks for which AI simply is not the right choice

There are areas in which AI performs surprisingly poorly, such as counting or carrying out highly complex tasks. It is therefore important to recognise the limitations of AI and not to use it for purposes for which it is not designed.

**Example: Bibliography**

Do not use AI to generate a bibliography or to arrange an existing one alphabetically. AI may add content or fabricate sources that do not exist. Instead, use a reference management software tool such as Citavi or EndNote, which are available to you with a [campus licence](http://Do).

### When it is the journey that matters

There are tasks that require your personal attention to ensure genuine progress. This is particularly relevant in the context of academic writing, where the process of articulating ideas through writing often clarifies their intended expression. The writing process facilitates the organisation of ideas and their refinement into a coherent structure.

The ability to write is developed through practical experience. This process cannot be delegated to artificial intelligence. Mastering the writing process independently is not only essential, but also a fundamental learning outcome of academic work and a key requirement of good academic practice.

There are, however, ways to [support your writing process with AI](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/ai-in-academic-writing/).
