# Good Scientific Practice

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/good-scientific-practice/*

The use of artificial intelligence in degree programmes is fundamentally guided by the existing principles of good academic practice. These apply irrespective of whether digital tools, traditional resources or AI-supported systems are used.

### A concise overview of good scientific practice

All scientific work is based on good scientific practice, which ensures that scientific achievements are carried out independently, transparently and honestly.

Key principles of good scientific practice

- Independence: Academic achievements must be accomplished independently.
- Honesty: Other people's contributions – including those made by AI – must not be passed off as your own work.
- Transparency: The use of aids must be disclosed
- Traceability: The process of creating scientific work must remain verifiable.

The principles of good scientific practice also apply without restriction to the use of artificial intelligence and form the basis for dealing with AI in scientific work at the University of Vienna. In addition, you must also respect copyright and data protection regulations.
