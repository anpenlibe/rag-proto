### Studying safely - an overview

## Emergency text message for students

The emergency text message service notifies you directly via text message in case of a sudden emergency (such as a major fire) at a location of the University of Vienna. It thus helps ensure safe university operations.

### How does it work?

Students can register for the emergency text message service on a voluntary basis with their private phone number on [u:space](https://uspace.univie.ac.at/web/studium/notfall-sms). You can deregister from the service at any time.

Further information such as about data protection is available on [u:space](https://uspace.univie.ac.at/web/studium/notfall-sms) and in the [Data Protection Declaration of the University of Vienna](https://dsba.univie.ac.at/fileadmin/user_upload/p_dsba/datenschutzerklaerung_websites_V04_26062020_EN.pdf).

### Procedure in case of student accidents

What should I do if a fellow student or I have an accident while studying?

Accidents have to be reported. Please note that accident reports concerning students in connection with their studies must be submitted electronically. We ask you to please NO longer use any previous PDF forms or copy templates that you may have saved.

The most recent version of the form “Accident report for persons in educational institutions” can be used by students who have had an accident at the university or on the way to or from the university. It is completed via the AUVA website ([auva.at/unfallmeldung](http://auva.at/unfallmeldung)).

1. Students fill out the form and select the option “Forward at the educational institution”.
2. Students enter the name and email address of the director of studies. If the accident occurred in a service facility, e.g. in the university library, USI, etc., then the email goes to the DLE management there. In cases of doubt, the report can also be sent to the management of the DLE Study Services and Teaching ([slw.leitung@univie.ac.at](mailto:slw.leitung@univie.ac.at)).
3. The head of the department or the head of the division will be notified and will then forward this form electronically to the AUVA.
4. If you have any questions about the accident report, the AUVA service team will be happy to help at any time.
   Contact the AUVA at:
   E-Mail: [wla@auva.at](mailto:wla@auva.at), Tel.: +43 5 9393 31383 (for Vienna, Lower Austria, Burgenland).

### Do not give thieves a chance!

Do not leave your personal belongings unattended in public areas of the University of Vienna.

This also applies to Student Spaces and course rooms.
In the event of theft, you can report it to any police station.
