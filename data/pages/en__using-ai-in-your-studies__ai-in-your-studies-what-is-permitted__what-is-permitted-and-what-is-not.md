# What is permitted? And what is not?

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/ai-in-your-studies-what-is-permitted/what-is-permitted-and-what-is-not/*

Whether and how you may use AI in a course or in an examination is determined by the learning outcomes, the fundamental [principles of good academic practice](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/good-scientific-practice/#c1269035) and the requirements set by the respective lecturer. Therefore, always check out

- the information in u:find
- notices on Moodle
- information on specific assignments and examinations

In the event of ambiguity surrounding the use of AI, it is advisable to consult with the lecturer in advance to establish clear regulations. In instances where there is uncertainty, it is prudent to assume that AI is not permitted unless explicit permission has been obtained.

### This is usually permitted:

AI may be used, provided that responsibility for the content is retained and the independent nature of the work is preserved. For example, AI may be used to:

- generate ideas or structure topics
- gain an overview of a new topic or prepare for reading
- revise the language of one's own texts
- support reflection or check comprehensibility
- find examples or alternatives which are reviewed and contextualised independently

The use of AI must always be compatible with the requirements set by the lecturer and must not replace your independent contribution.

### This is usually prohibited:

The use of AI is prohibited in cases where it undermines the required competencies or where its use has not been disclosed. It is therefore prohibited to

- submit AI-generated texts or solutions as your own work
- allow AI to complete assignments, analyses or arguments in full or to a substantial extent
- use AI in examinations or other forms of assessment where this has not expressly been permitted
- adopt content generated by AI without indicating this where transparency is required
- use fabricated sources, quotations or bibliographies without verification

Such use may be regarded as a breach of the [principles of good academic practice](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/good-scientific-practice/#c1269035) or as cheating.
