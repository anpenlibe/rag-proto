# The day of the written test

The big day for your degree programme with entrance exam procedure/aptitude test is just around the corner. But what actually happens on a test day? On this page, you will find information about the procedure, permitted and prohibited items, and all the important rules for the day.

## Getting to the written test

The University of Vienna holds the written admission and aptitude tests at the [VIECON – Vienna Congress & Convention Center](https://vieconcenter.at/en/). Please visit the [Wiener Linien website](https://www.wienerlinien.at/web/wl-en) for information on how to get there and tickets for public transport.

You can find the admission time and the expected end of the test on your permission to attend (“Einlassbestätigung”). However, we recommend that you keep the entire day free. Please be punctual and, if necessary, arrive the day before. If you arrive too late, you will be excluded from the entrance exam/aptitude test! There are no alternative dates.

## Guidelines on items

You are allowed to bring a bag to the written test. This bag must not be larger than hand luggage. During the test, all prohibited items must remain in your bag without exception. Only explicitly permitted items are allowed to be on your desk.

If you use a prohibited item during the test, this will count as an attempt to cheat. In this case you will be excluded from the test.

- ### Required and permitted items

  The following items are **required** at your seat:

  - printout of the permission to attend (“Einlassbestätigung”) in the format A4 (available via u:space > Personal matters > My documents)
  - valid passport or personal ID card

  The following items are **permitted** at your seat:

  - drinks and snacks
  - recommended: sweater/jacket/vest (the test venue is air-conditioned)
- ### Prohibited items

  - your own paper, sticky notes
  - white out pens, erasers, rulers
  - pens of any kind (including highlighters)
  - electronic objects (calculator, mobile phone, tablet, smartwatch, headphones, etc.)
  - watches of any kind (there are watches in the test hall)
  - study materials, dictionaries, encyclopaedias, formula collections, periodic tables, etc.
  - hearing protections
  - (E-)cigarettes, vapes, matches, lighters, purse, keys

## Procedure on the day of the written test

## Step 1: Entry at the VIECON

Please note: Only those with a permission to attend (printed in A4 format!) are allowed to enter the VIECON. In this permission and in the access plan you will find information about your access to the test hall. The entry is organised in stages via several entrances.

Only use the entrance intended for you! We will check your identity. Therefore, please have your passport or personal ID card and your printed permission to attend on hand.

## Step 2: Entry and your seat

- ### Entry to the test room

  The personalized QR code on your permission to attend will be scanned at the entrance to the test hall. You are not allowed to leave the test hall after that. Toilets are located in the test hall. Please note: Smoking is not permitted.
- ### Personalized seat

  Your seat is personalized and can only be accessed via the entrance assigned to you. Please proceed directly to your allocated sector. Supervisory staff will help you find your sector and seat. You may leave your own seat to go to the toilet, but you may not go to any other seats.

## Step 3: The written test

Before the test, you will receive detailed instructions on how to proceed (for example, how to mark your answers or how to correct incorrectly marked answers). The exact end time of the test will also be announced.

Take a look at the sample exercises and possible questions for the tests on our test details website.

## Step 4: After the written test

For organizational reasons, you may not leave the test hall early. Please take this into account when planning your schedule and travel arrangements. Upon request, you can obtain time confirmations (for military service, civil service, etc.) after the written test.

We will inform you of your test result and your next steps in the admission procedure via e-mail.

### I need electronic devices for health reasons. How should I proceed?

If you need an adaptation of the written test, this must be stated when you apply for admission. You did not do so and need an electronic device for health reasons? Please contact us as soon as possible via the [Servicedesk](https://servicedesk.univie.ac.at/plugins/servlet/desk/portal/62/create/929). Let us know exactly which devices you need. A diagnosis is not required.

[Information about the entrance exam procedure for applicants with disabilities](https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/entrance-exam/entrance-exam-procedures-for-applicants-with-disabilities/)

### Can I also show my QR code on my smartphone?

No, this is not possible. You need to bring your printed permission to attend in the format A4.
