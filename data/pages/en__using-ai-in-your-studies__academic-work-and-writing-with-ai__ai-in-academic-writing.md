## Why academic writing?

Academic writing is a crucial component of degree programmes, as it demonstrates an individual's ability to understand, contextualise and further develop subject-specific content. The finished text makes visible an individual's capacity to present coherent arguments, handle sources correctly and apply the disciplinary language of their field of study.

However, it is important to note that the final product, i.e. the completed text, is not the only decisive factor. The writing process itself is an essential learning tool: By formulating, structuring and revising, you develop your academic thinking, refine concepts and make reasoned decisions. This is precisely where the central learning gain of academic work lies.

The process of writing allows for the generation of new ideas, the refinement of arguments, and the enhancement of connections between ideas. The ambiguity of one's own line of argument often becomes evident only when attempting to express it in language. Errors and revisions should not be seen as indications of failure, but rather as integral components of the learning process. Each revision contributes to the sharpening of concepts, the improvement of argumentative structures, and the enhancement of the clarity of one's position.

As a consequence, academic writing always also supports the development of your own academic skills.

### That implies:

Writing is not only a means of presenting knowledge, but also an essential tool for understanding and critically reflecting on scientific content.

### Content-related and linguistic-formal tasks

In academic writing, a distinction is made between Higher Order Concerns (HOCs) and Later Order Concerns (LOCs).

- Higher-order concerns relate to the substantive and conceptual level of a text. These include developing a viable research question, structuring a coherent line of argument or interpreting results. These aspects constitute the core of independent academic work.
- Later-order concerns refer to the linguistic and formal presentation of the text. These include grammar, style, readability and correct citation.

Beide Ebenen sind für einen gelungenen wissenschaftlichen Text gleichermaßen wichtig; die Unterscheidung bezieht sich jedoch vor allem auf den **Zeitpunkt im Schreibprozess**, da sprachlich-formale Aspekte sinnvollerweise erst dann im Detail überarbeitet werden, wenn die inhaltlich-konzeptionelle Struktur des Textes tragfähig ausgearbeitet ist.

Both levels are equally important for a successful academic text; however, the distinction primarily concerns the stage within the writing process when these tasks are addressed: linguistic and formal aspects are most effectively revised in detail once the substantive and conceptual structure of the text has been developed.

The distinction between Higher and Later Order Concerns can also guide you in your use of AI:

- AI can be used effectively to support linguistic and formal tasks.
- With regard to higher-order concerns, particular attention is required: AI may provide impulses or contribute to reflection, but it must not make substantive decisions, develop lines of argument or provide interpretations.

What is decisive is that intellectual responsibility for the text always remains with the author and that the independent academic contribution is clearly [recognisable](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/citing-and-labelling-ai/).

## Use Cases of AI in scientific writing

AI tools can support you at different stages of academic writing. It is important to use AI as a tool within the writing process and not as a substitute for your own subject-specific engagement. How AI may support your writing depends on the stage of writing you are currently in.

### Caution when using AI for literature research

Important: Do not use generative AI tools such as ChatGPT or u:ai for research. These tools do not access databases and may fabricate sources. Instead, use dedicated literature research tools such as Elicit, Consensus or others.

The library offers specialised training sessions on [literature research with AI](http://urise.univie.ac.at/course/index.php?categoryid=6&wbtsearch=ai+literacy).

### Prompting tip: How to formulate prompts that ensure your own contribution

Write your prompts in a way that AI does not think or write for you, but supports you in your own thinking. Ask for questions, alternatives, feedback or suggestions for structure, rather than for completed texts or substantive decisions. Effective prompts typically:

- assign AI a clearly supportive role (for example feedback or assistance in reflection),
- explicitly state what AI should not do (for example provide ready-made arguments),
- make it clear that selection, evaluation and further development remain your responsibility.

It is important that you regard the suggestions as a starting point and review, contextualise and further develop them.

- Before writing: Orientation and preparation

  In the early stages of a writing project, AI can help organize thoughts and find initial approaches to a topic. It can provide inspiration, but it cannot replace independent decision-making on content.

  As a general rule, formulate AI queries in such a way that the AI highlights possibilities and provides feedback, while you remain responsible for selection, evaluation, and further development.

  Possible areas of application:

  - Generating ideas: Formulate your request in such a way that the AI provides several different thought-provoking ideas without evaluating or selecting content. Ask for brief explanations of the respective perspectives, not for detailed arguments or text passages.
  - Develop and narrow down topic suggestions: Ensure that the AI highlights possible variations and distinctions rather than specifying a topic. The request should make it clear that the decision regarding focus, scope, and relevance is yours.
  - Create suggestions for research questions: Ask the AI to suggest several possible questions and reveal their implicit assumptions. Avoid asking for “the best” or “most suitable” research question, as scientific evaluation must be carried out independently.
  - Help develop arguments (e.g., in the form of a [Socratic dialogue](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/questions-instead-of-answers-the-socratic-dialogue/)): Formulate your request in such a way that the AI asks questions, identifies counterarguments, or points out possible weaknesses without formulating a line of argumentation itself or suggesting conclusions.
  - translate foreign-language texts
  - Convert your own spoken thoughts into text (e.g., using the dictation function from Microsoft or Google, [Whisper Wizard](https://www.whisperwizard.com/)): Make sure that the AI processes your spoken thoughts in a structured and linguistically organized manner, but does not add to the content. The request should make it clear that unclear or fragmentary passages may remain visible.

  Tip: You can also have AI tools read texts aloud to you (e.g., Textreader, Pickles).
- During writing: Structure and feedback

  During the writing process, AI can be used for structuring and providing selective feedback on text passages.

  Possible areas of application:

  - Suggestions for outlines or tables of contents: Formulate your request in such a way that the AI shows several possible structural variants and explains their internal logic. Avoid requests for a finished or optimal outline.
  - Tips for ensuring individual paragraphs are easy to understand: Ask the AI to highlight any ambiguities, long sentence structures, or potential obstacles to comprehension. The request should be aimed at obtaining feedback on readability, not at rephrasing or expanding content.
  - Feedback on text context or argumentation logic: Structure your request in such a way that the AI highlights breaks, jumps, or missing transitions in the text. Avoid requests that require restructuring or adding content arguments. The goal is to review your own argumentation, not to have it developed by the AI.
  - Support with literature overviews: Formulate your request in such a way that the AI supports you with organizational tasks, such as clustering topics or summarizing abstracts. The request should make it clear that the selection, evaluation, and classification of the literature is done independently and that the AI does not make any decisions regarding relevance. Please note the information on [literature research](#c1268854).

  Even during the writing process, AI should be used in such a way that it provides guidance and feedback without taking over key decisions regarding content or lines of argumentation, or formulating text for you.
- Revision: Language and formal precision

  During the revision phase, AI can be particularly helpful with linguistic and formal aspects. This support relates to the presentation of the text, not its content.

  Possible areas of application:

  - Feedback on language, style, and text context: Formulate your request in such a way that the AI provides feedback on linguistic quality and readability without changing the content or introducing new arguments. Ask it to highlight problematic passages and suggest improvements rather than completely rewriting the text.
  - Spelling and correction assistance: Ensure that the request relates exclusively to orthographic correctness. The AI should correct spelling mistakes, typos, or obvious formal inaccuracies without changing the sentence structure or meaning. Tip: Don't let the AI formulate a corrected version of the text; instead, ask for a list of points to be corrected. Then you can decide for yourself whether and how to accept the suggested corrections.
  - Word choice and phrasing assistance: AI can assist you with word choice and phrasing. Specifically, AI can help you with suggestions for synonyms, rephrasing, or suggestions for variations of a sentence with different linguistic registers. Structure your query so that the AI suggests alternative expressions or synonyms that are stylistically and technically appropriate. Avoid queries that could lead to simplifications in content or shifts in meaning. The decision on which wording to use remains yours.
  - Check grammar and punctuation: Ask the AI to identify grammatical errors and punctuation mistakes. The request should make it clear that no changes are to be made to the content and that the technical meaning remains unchanged.

  ####

  #### Tip: Lists instead of finished text

  Don't let the AI formulate a corrected version of the text; instead, ask for a list of the points that need to be corrected and why. Then decide for yourself whether and how you want to accept the suggested corrections.

  The key is: carefully review all suggestions to maintain technical accuracy and your own writing style. Do not uncritically accept all corrections or comments suggested by the AI.

  Suitable tools: ChatGPT, DeepL Write, Grammarly

AI can support the writing process at all stages. In tasks that relate to content-related argumentation, interpretation or evaluation, it is essential to use AI with restraint and reflection.

### Always follow the teacher's instructions!

In the context of academic writing, it is the responsibility of the lecturer to determine the permitted use of aids. Any tools that are not explicitly permitted are prohibited. Prior to commencing work on your text, it is essential to familiarise yourself with the documentation requirements for the use of text-generating AI and other aids. It is imperative to take [data protection considerations](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/ai-in-your-studies-what-is-permitted/legal-requirements-data-protection-and-copyright/#c1269046) into account at all times.
