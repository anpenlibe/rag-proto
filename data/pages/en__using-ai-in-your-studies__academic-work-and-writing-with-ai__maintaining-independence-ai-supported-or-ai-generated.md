# Maintaining independence: AI-generated or AI-supported?

*Section: Study organisation · Language: en · Source: https://studieren.univie.ac.at/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/maintaining-independence-ai-supported-or-ai-generated/*

When you use AI in your studies, what is decisive is the extent of your own contribution. A helpful point of reference is the [distinction between AI-generated content and AI-supported work](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/doceo/document/TA-9-2020-0277_DE.html). This distinction can help you assess:

- what qualifies as your own work,
- what may be problematic,
- and how you should render the use of AI transparent.

## AI-generated scientific content

The practice of directly sourcing passages or entire texts from an AI tool without substantial revision constitutes an infringement of academic integrity. In such instances, the fundamental decisions originate from the AI system, with the individual's contribution being confined to providing the prompt. This practice is in direct contravention of the established principles of academic integrity and may be problematic, particularly if it is not disclosed.

## AI as a Tool

For a text produced with the assistance of AI to qualify as your own work, there must be a substantial human contribution that decisively shapes the form of the generated text. In this case, the AI tool merely serves in a supportive capacity as an executing instrument. This applies:

- where a highly sophisticated prompt is used, which you have created yourself and whose formulation may require relevant knowledge;
- where text is produced through the combination of strongly directive prompts that determine the essential structural decisions rather than leaving them to the AI tool;
- where users further develop the generated text as a stimulus for reflection (cf. [Salden und Leschke 2023](https://hss-opus.ub.ruhr-uni-bochum.de/opus4/frontdoor/deliver/index/docId/9734/file/2023_03_06_Didaktik_Recht_KI_Hochschulbildung.pdf))

In these cases, AI complements the academic working process without replacing your independent contribution. What is crucial is that the substantive decisions remain yours. This is the case, for example, when you use AI:

- to generate ideas or as a stimulus for reflection;
- to obtain structural or outline suggestions that form the basis for further development (not for direct 1:1 adoption);
- as a tool for reflection, for example to review arguments;
- or to revise the language of your own texts.

It is imperative that you critically examine, develop and embed AI-generated results within your own line of argument. The more actively you guide AI and the more decisions you take yourself, the clearer your independent contribution remains.

## Controlling AI in a targeted manner and maintaining independence

The quality of AI-generated output depends to a large extent on how precisely and reflectively a request is formulated. AI tools do not independently respond to academic requirements; rather, they orient themselves to the specifications they receive through [prompts](/en/using-ai-in-your-studies/academic-work-and-writing-with-ai/controlling-ai-in-a-targeted-manner-the-importance-of-prompting-correctly/).

It is important to define the objective, context and framework conditions clearly, as this will ensure the most effective use of AI as a supportive tool. Through targeted prompting, the user retains control over the working process, determines the direction and depth of responses, and ensures that substantive decisions remain theirs. AI thus provides impulses – the academic contextualisation and further development remain the responsibility of the user.

### Prompt-Tipp: So formulieren Sie Prompts, die Ihre Eigenleistung sichern

Formulieren Sie Prompts so, dass die KI **nicht für Sie denkt oder schreibt**, sondern Sie beim eigenen Denken unterstützt. Bitten Sie um **Fragen, Alternativen, Rückmeldungen oder Strukturvorschläge**, nicht um fertige Texte oder inhaltliche Entscheidungen. Bewährt haben sich Prompts, die:

- die KI auf eine **unterstützende Rolle** festlegen (zB Feedback, Reflexionshilfe),
- klar benennen, **was die KI nicht tun soll** (zB keine fertigen Argumente),
- deutlich machen, dass **Auswahl, Bewertung und Weiterverarbeitung** bei Ihnen liegen.

**Dabei gilt:** Die Vorschläge dienen als Ausgangspunkt und müssen fachlich geprüft, eingeordnet und weiterentwickelt werden.
